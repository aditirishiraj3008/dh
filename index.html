S A N D

It is estimated that, by 2050, the human population will jump to a whopping 9.8 billion!

To house all of these people tons and tons of concrete, sand and other aggregates will be needed.

Currently for construction riverbed sand, marine sand and manufacturing sand are used.

Dredging of riverbeds causes huge damage to the habitat, leading to loss of biodiversity and soil erosion.

Illegal sand extraction and trade come in the top 3 transnational crimes.

Illegal sand mining cases in North Karnataka, which have devastated rivers, have gone up from 4,402 in 2022-23 to 5,441 in 2023-24.

Our world is covered in sand, desert sand to be exact. Deserts cover more than one-fifth of Earth's land area, and they are found on every continent. Desert sand is largely useless to us.

Desert sand grains are in the wrong shape. Eroded by wind rather than water, they are too smooth and rounded to lock together to be used for construction.

Our Solution...

We decided to engineer Bacillus subtilis bacteria to produce an exopolysaccharide, γ-PGA. To further strengthen our newly engineered sand we will be using MICP (Microbially Induced Calcite Precipitation).
